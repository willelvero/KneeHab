The Problem

"Cleared" Doesn't
Mean Prepared.

Pain-free doesn't mean ready. You can be medically cleared and still lack the things that actually let you trust your body:

  • Confidence
  • Stability
  • Force Absorption
  • Athleticism
  • Trust In Your Body

That's why so many people never truly return to the level they know they're capable of. Most rehab stops when pain decreases or just when they're a normal, walking human again — but in order to come back better than ever…

That's where our work comes in.

Our Solution

Our 3 Pillars To Coming Back Better Than Before

1

Frequency Is Your Best Friend.

Most people are not training nearly enough. It's simple:

More training = More movement
More movement = More blood flow
More blood flow = More healing

Think about brushing your teeth. To get nice, healthy pearly whites you brush for one minute every day — that adds up to about 365 minutes a year. Now imagine you spent a straight 24 hours brushing your teeth nonstop, then didn't brush again for the other 364 days. Your breath would stink and your teeth would be falling out — even though that's 1,440 minutes, roughly 4× the time of brushing one minute a day. Point is… Intensity ≠ Consistency

2

The Knee Is Rarely the Actual Problem.

The knee is often the symptom, not the root cause. Most people spend all of their time focusing on the knee, but in reality the knees often pay the price from something else not doing its job.

The body works as one connected system. So instead of only focusing on the knees, we identify and strengthen the weak links throughout the entire chain.

  1. 01 Feet & Ankles Your First Line of Defense

    Every step starts at the ground, and your feet and ankles are the first thing to absorb the impact. If they can't control that force, it travels straight up to the knee. After an injury the foot-and-ankle complex — including the soleus, calves, and tibialis — is usually ignored, but it's the foundation for your stability, balance, and how efficiently you transfer power. Rebuild the base and you take a huge amount of unnecessary stress off the knee.

  2. 02 Quads & Hamstrings Generates Strength & Power

    The quad is usually the first muscle to shut down after knee surgery — sometimes you can't even flex it for a week or two. That isn't only weakness; the brain temporarily loses the signal to fire it. Rebuilding that mind-muscle connection through frequent, consistent reps is one of the biggest levers in recovery. Strong, responsive quads and hamstrings produce and control the force that protects the joint through every movement.

  3. 03 Hips The Engine & The Most Overlooked Muscle Group

    The hips are one of the most overlooked groups in knee recovery. Tight or weak hip flexors and glutes change how your pelvis sits and how your leg tracks, which dumps excess stress straight onto the knee. Strong hips stabilize the pelvis, drive the legs when you walk and run, and keep the whole limb moving efficiently — so the knee isn't compensating for the work the hips should be doing.

  4. 04 Core & Lower Back Your Center of Control

    Your core and lower back are the center of control. They keep your trunk stable so force can transfer cleanly from the ground, through the hips, and into your movement. When the core is weak, that force leaks and the knee ends up absorbing what the system should be sharing. A strong, controlled center lets the entire chain work as one unit instead of overloading any single joint.

  5. 05 Knees The Victim When the Rest of the Chain Is Undertrained

    The knee is almost never the actual root cause — it's just where the problem shows up. It sits in the middle of the chain, so when the foundation below or the engine above isn't doing its job, the knee pays the price. That's why we don't chase knee symptoms in isolation. We strengthen everything above and below it, so the joint is supported and protected from both directions.

3

Confidence Comes from Preparation.

Confidence doesn't come from timeline — it comes from preparation. The more prepared your body is for the demands of real life and sport, the more confidence you'll naturally develop.

  1. 01 Lifts Train movements and positions that emulate real life movements

    Confidence comes from preparation, and preparation means training the way your body actually moves in life and sport. A back-loaded squat with your weight on your heels looks nothing like cutting, jumping, or sprinting. We prioritize single-leg work, split squats, and athletic patterns trained through full ranges of motion — so the strength you build transfers directly to the demands you'll face.

  2. 02 Force Absorption Learning to land and decelerate

    Around 80% of knee injuries happen during landing and deceleration — the eccentric phase. Most programs only train how to produce force; we teach your body how to absorb it first. Through landing mechanics, deceleration drills, eccentric strength, and reflexive control, you build the brakes your knee needs before we ever build the gas pedal.

  3. 03 Force Redirection Turning absorbed force into the next move

    Once you can absorb force, the next step is redirecting it — taking that energy and channeling it into a new direction. This is where change-of-direction, cutting, and reactive plyometric work come in. It bridges the gap between absorbing a landing and exploding into the next movement, which is exactly what real life and sport demand.

  4. 04 Force Production Building the speed and power back

    With a body that can absorb and redirect force safely, we build the output: speed, power, explosiveness, and athleticism. This is where return-to-sport confidence is earned — not by being cleared, but by proving to yourself your body can do everything it could before, and more.

FAQ

Still Have Questions?

What kinds of injuries do you work with?

Everything — ACL, MCL, LCL, meniscus, patellar tendonitis, runner's knee, post-surgical stiffness, chronic instability, even arthritis and "bone-on-bone."

Because the body works as one connected chain, we also work with people with feet/ankle, back, and hip injuries/issues.

How is this different from physical therapy?

We are not against PT, but the difference is the goal and how we get there. PT's goal is to get you "cleared" and they typically do so through a general roadmap or timeline they give to everyone.

Our goal is to get you stronger, more confident, and more athletic than before the injury — by fixing YOUR specific root cause. We are going to pinpoint where you are personally weakest at and make you a personal roadmap to address your weak points throughout the whole chain, not just the knee.

Can I do this alongside PT?

Absolutely! A lot of our clients do BOTH. We build around your PT schedule.

The KneeHab program was actually originally designed to do alongside physical therapy.

I've already been cleared by PT. Can I still do this?

Perfect timing. PT usually wraps once you're "cleared" for daily life — and that's exactly where most people get stuck: the knee "works," but you don't feel strong or athletic. You aren't confident in it. That gap between where you are now and where you want to be is what we're built for.

Our goal is for you to come back better than you were before.

I'm not a pro athlete — I just want to live without pain.

This is absolutely still for you. Most of our clients aren't chasing a podium — they just want to walk downstairs, hike, keep up with their kids, or play weekend pickup without babying the knee. The training scales to your goals, whatever they are.

Do I need any equipment?

Nope! We actually ship you our resistance bands. Your program will be written with whatever you have access to. If you want to workout from home, we will make that happen. If you have access to a gym we will make sure our plan includes exercises you can do at your gym. Either or, we will make sure your circumstances never hold you back.

Do I need surgery? Is it too late if my injury was years ago?

Neither's a dealbreaker. We work with people at every stage — prehab, fresh post-op, years out, and plenty who NEVER had surgery. And it's never too late: many of our clients have been dealing with knee pain for 5, 10, even 20+ years before we worked together. An old injury just means the surrounding muscles have been compensating a long time — exactly what we fix.

How does the coaching work?

You get a personal coach based around your injury, personality, and goals that will program a fully customized plan in an app. You log your workouts, your coach tracks the data to find your weak links, and you hop on Zoom calls as often as you need to adjust. You also get a full education library and weekly group Q&A calls.

Can I incorporate upper body or my own training?

Absolutely. Your plan will be built around your goals, schedule, equipment, and current training plans — whether that's upper-body, running, or sport-specific work. Some clients will send us their splits and we'll build around it, others have us program everything for them head to toe.

I'm really busy — how much time does this take?

We tailor our schedule around YOU. That means your training can be as little or as much as you want. We normally cycle high and low volume days — "high day" sessions run 45–70 minutes, "low days" are a 10–20 minute workout you can knock out first thing in the morning or before bed.

Worried about being busy on a random Tuesday or going on vacation? No problem. Just talk with your coach, we will rearrange your schedule to make it work. At the end of the day, consistency beats intensity every time.

How soon will I see results?

Many people notice a difference within the first 2 weeks — usually from finally training consistently and training the whole chain, not just the knee. But our goal isn't just helping you "feel better." Our goal is helping you come back better than ever before!

Typically, long-lasting, meaningful results can take 3–6 months. We won't promise a date, but it does compound fast.
